Great White Shark

The great white shark is ovoviviparous, which means that the shark grows in an egg, which is then hatched inside the mother.

  • Scientific Name: Carcharodon carcharias
  • Average Length: 15 - 21 ft.
  • Average Lifespan: 70 years
  • Habitat: The outskirts of shore waters.

The great white shark has no known natural predators other than, on very rare occasions, the killer whale. The great white shark is arguably the world's largest known extant macropredatory fish, and is one of the primary predators of marine mammals. It is also known to prey upon a variety of other marine animals, including fish and seabirds.
